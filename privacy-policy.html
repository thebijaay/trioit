3.4 Legal Basis for Processing Framework

We process personal data based on the following legal grounds, as defined by applicable data protection regulations:

1
Explicit Consent
Processing based on clear, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent provided voluntarily. Consent can be withdrawn at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.
2
Contractual Necessity
Processing necessary for the performance of a contract to which the data subject is party or to take steps at the request of the data subject prior to entering into a contract.
3
Legitimate Interests
Processing necessary for the purposes of legitimate interests pursued by the controller or by a third party, except where such interests are overridden by the interests or fundamental rights and freedoms of the data subject.
4
Legal Obligations
Processing necessary for compliance with legal obligations to which the controller is subject under applicable laws and regulations.
5
Vital Interests
Processing necessary to protect the vital interests of the data subject or another natural person in emergency situations.
6
Public Interest
Processing necessary for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest or in the exercise of official authority vested in the controller.

7. Comprehensive Data Subject Rights Framework

Empowering Data Subjects: We provide extensive rights and control mechanisms for individuals regarding their personal information, with clear processes for exercising these rights.
Right to Access & Information
You can request a comprehensive copy of all personal data we hold about you, including processing purposes, categories of data, recipients, retention periods, and the source of the data.
Right to Rectification
You can request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data. We will rectify the data and notify any recipients to whom the data has been disclosed.
Right to Erasure (Right to Be Forgotten)
You can request deletion of your personal data under specific conditions, including when the data is no longer necessary, consent is withdrawn, or processing is unlawful.
Right to Restriction of Processing
You can request temporary restriction of processing while accuracy is verified, processing is unlawful, data is no longer needed but required for legal claims, or you object to processing.
Right to Data Portability
You can receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format and transmit it to another controller where processing is based on consent or contract.
Right to Object
You can object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes. We will cease processing unless we demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests.

8. International Data Transfer Framework

As a global AI and cybersecurity service provider, your personal information may be processed in countries other than your country of residence. These countries may have data protection laws that are different from the laws of your country. We implement comprehensive safeguards for all international data transfers.

8.1 Transfer Mechanisms and Safeguards

We ensure adequate protection for international data transfers through multiple mechanisms:

Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
Implementation of EU-approved Standard Contractual Clauses for data transfers from the European Economic Area to third countries. Our SCCs include additional safeguards and supplementary measures as required by the Schrems II ruling.
Adequacy Decisions
Transfer to countries with adequacy decisions from the European Commission or equivalent recognition from other regulatory bodies. We maintain a current list of adequacy-recognized countries and ensure transfers comply with recognition criteria.
Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)
Development and implementation of Binding Corporate Rules for intra-organizational transfers, approved by relevant data protection authorities where applicable.
Technical & Organizational Measures
Implementation of additional technical measures including end-to-end encryption, pseudonymization, and access controls, along with organizational measures such as data protection impact assessments and regular audits.
